Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about business insurance, brokers, claims, and how Reliant can help protect your business.

Business insurance can be complicated. A broker helps assess and manage risks, leveraging their technical expertise of insurance markets, prices, terms and conditions. They help arrange, acquire and maintain your insurance coverage while acting as your advocate during claims. With a broker, you gain a partner who understands your business needs and negotiates on your behalf.

Yes. The risk of running uninsured far outweighs the cost. Unexpected events can lead to companies going out of business. Key risks include: inability to afford compensation claims, loss of income, reduced profits, inability to repair damaged goods, and legal cases against your personal assets. Business insurance provides essential protection and peace of mind.

Subject to your coverage, you can claim for: thefts, claims from employees, customers or visitors due to negligence, damage to property from fires, floods, or water leaks, and loss of revenue during the repair period (business interruption). The specific circumstances covered depend on your policy details, so it's important to review your coverage with your broker.

If the incident is serious, call the police or emergency services first. Then advise your broker as soon as possible. Complete a claim form promptly and provide: the time, date and location of the incident, a detailed description of what happened, steps you've taken to minimise loss, an estimate of the loss, eyewitness contact details, and photographs of the damage. Quick action helps expedite the claims process.
