Social Media Risks for Your Business

Back to News
May 27, 2021
Cyber & Digital

Social media is everywhere. It is unavoidable, it is powerful, and it is here to stay. That being said, social media does come with a set of risks. Industry research shows that a majority of organisations experience material or significant cyber incidents each year, and social media represents a growing channel for these threats.

It is crucial to recognise that with social media, messages can spread far and wide very quickly. While this is one of the reasons social media marketing is so valued, when defamation, slander, and negative publicity are involved, it can cause serious damage to the reputation of a business. A negative brand image will have a lasting impact until resolved effectively with proper risk management techniques.

Social Media Policies and Guidelines

Have a clear process as to who is responsible for posting on social media. Establish guidelines for what messages are appropriate and the tone and voice to be used. This process should also provide guidance on how often social media is monitored and responded to, and especially how to respond to negative comments in a professional manner.

Employee Training

Human error accounts for a significant portion of cyber attacks. In today's busy world, it is all too easy for an employee to accidentally expose the company to threats online. Ensuring your employees are involved in relevant training around social media is key. Train staff on how to identify and avoid scams, attacks, and other security threats, who to notify, and how to respond if a social media security concern arises.

Professional vs Personal Use

Always be wary of the language you use on social media. When deciding on what social channels to use for your business, take into account the needs of the organisation, not personal preferences. Remember that nothing on social media is really private. It is important to take the time to think through a message before sharing it.

What Can Insurance Cover?

If your business has been accused of defamation as a result of comments made on social media, cover may be available within public and product liability insurance, professional indemnity insurance, as well as certain other policies. These policies may also help cover legal costs required in defending yourself in court if your business has been targeted by malicious online activity.

The digital world is not 100% secure and even with the best antivirus software and firewalls, cyber threats are real. With numerous social media accounts and the use of third-party apps for creating content and scheduling posts, attackers have more avenues to breach business data records, intellectual property, and deploy ransomware. Having cyber insurance could be invaluable in these situations.
