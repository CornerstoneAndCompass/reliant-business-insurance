What is Volunteer Insurance?

Back to News
Aug 27, 2021
Specialist Insurance

Volunteer insurance protects both organisations and their volunteer workers from accidents and liability. This coverage is essential for charities, nonprofits, community groups, religious organisations, and event-hosting entities that rely on unpaid workers to deliver their services and programs.

Who Needs Volunteer Insurance?

Organisations relying on volunteer labour require this specialised coverage. This includes community groups, healthcare charities, religious institutions, recreation clubs, and event organisers. Standard business policies typically do not protect volunteers since they differ fundamentally from salaried employees. Volunteer insurance shields both the volunteers themselves and the organisations from public liability claims arising from volunteer activities.

Key Statistics on Volunteering in Australia

Volunteering plays a vital role in Australian society and the economy:

  • An estimated six million volunteers work across Australia
  • The Australian economy gains approximately $290 billion from volunteer contributions annually
  • Volunteering participation spans all ages and genders, with those aged 40 to 54 representing the largest volunteer demographic

Given the scale of volunteer activity, ensuring proper insurance coverage is not just prudent but essential for the organisations that coordinate these efforts.

Personal Accident Coverage

Personal accident coverage protects volunteers who are injured during authorised activities. This typically includes weekly payments during the recovery period to help cover lost income, as well as medical expense coverage for treatment related to the injury. This coverage provides peace of mind for volunteers who might otherwise be reluctant to participate without protection.

Public Liability Coverage

Public liability coverage protects organisations, their employees, and their volunteers against third-party injury or property damage claims. If a volunteer accidentally causes harm to a member of the public or damages property while performing their volunteer duties, this coverage responds to protect both the volunteer and the organisation from potentially devastating legal costs.

Voluntary Board Coverage

Professional Indemnity Liability shields directors and board members of volunteer organisations from negligence, defamation, and harassment claims. This coverage is not typically included in standard policies but is critically important for those who serve in governance roles for charitable and community organisations.
